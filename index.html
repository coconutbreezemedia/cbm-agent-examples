01
Sophie James Wine · Influencer Marketing

An AI talent scout that thinks like the founder

We turned a winemaker's gut instinct for "which creator is actually worth a paid partnership" into a skeptical-by-default AI analyst that scores every candidate 1–5 — with reasons.

The opportunity

Sophie James Wine needed to find wine and lifestyle influencers worth partnering with — fast, at volume, and without lowering the bar. The hard part wasn't finding creators; it was judgment. A creator with a huge following can convert nobody, while a quiet LTK-native can outperform everyone. That instinct lived only in the founders' heads.

What we built

  • Sourcing at scale. Apify scrapes Instagram and Facebook by hashtag and search term (#LTK, "wine night"), geo-filtered to the US and automatically excluding states wine can't ship to.
  • Contact discovery on a cost ladder. A tiered escalation finds 4–5 emails per creator — free AI web search first, then Perplexity, then Firecrawl scraping the contact page — so cheap methods run before expensive ones.
  • An LLM rating layer that encodes founder intuition. A Claude scoring agent applies a weighted rubric — link clicks, conversion rate, average order value, follower band — with hard disqualifiers and a built-in blind-spot rule for creators who look weak on paper but convert best. Every score ships with a cited rationale and a confidence level.
  • Self-routing. 4–5 scores go to a human "Needs Review" queue; 2–3 auto-vet; 1s are rejected — so the team only ever looks at the strongest candidates.
The edge — The system is skeptical by default and caps its own confidence when conversion data is missing, so it never overstates. The #1 signal is link clicks, not follower count — the same metric the founders actually care about — wired straight into an Airtable + Make pipeline.

02
Travel Media Network · Partnership Sales

AI sales personas that run the whole outbound pipeline

Two AI "partnership reps" prospect, verify, personalize, and send — each with its own voice, signature, and a set of guardrails strict enough to keep a human comfortable handing over the wheel.

The opportunity

A travel-media partnerships team wanted the reach of a full outbound operation without the manual grind of research, list-cleaning, and one-off drafting. The goal: a system that could carry a real pipeline end to end while never sounding like a robot and never doing anything off-brand.

What we built

  • A full pipeline as slash commands. Find contacts (parallel research sub-agents by region and vertical) → vet (dedupe against the CRM, verify emails) → draft personalized outreach in a chosen persona → human approval → send. Every action is logged per contact.
  • Two distinct personas. Each has its own email, signature, knowledge base, and CC rules — so outreach stays consistent and on-message across the whole team.
  • Guardrails that earn trust. A hard per-address daily send cap, no sending during overnight hours (it would look automated), auto-send only on "safe" categories, and automatic escalation the moment anything touches money, contracts, or RFPs.
  • Migrated to be portable. Rebuilt from a bespoke Mac-mini orchestration onto Claude Code — skills, slash commands, and a portable memory system — then re-architected toward a per-rep copilot each salesperson can run with their own email and CRM.
The edge — Live in production and operated day-to-day, with the team's weekly capacity raised from a cautious pilot to full-scale use. The system enforces its own good behavior — down to refusing to suggest meeting times because a human owns the calendar.

Matador Network · Content Operations

One form turns any video into a publish-ready article and a creator script

Drop a TikTok, Reel, or YouTube link into a form and an autonomous pipeline scrapes it, transcribes it, and hands back two original pieces — written to never recycle the source.

The opportunity

Matador Network's team was spending hours turning short-form video into written content. They needed a way to go from "interesting clip" to "draft an editor can ship" without a person babysitting every step — and without producing thin rewrites of the original.

What we built

  • A 23-node n8n pipeline triggered by a single Airtable form submission, polling for new entries every minute.
  • Platform-aware ingestion. It routes by source — scraping YouTube, TikTok, or Instagram via the right Apify actor — and where there's no caption track, downloads the video and transcribes it with Whisper.
  • Two specialized AI writers. One drafts a 60–90 second green-screen creator script; the other writes an original 800-word article in Matador's voice. Both are explicitly instructed to be distinctly different from the source — no recycled hooks, no clichés.
  • Self-healing and hands-off. Drafts write back to Airtable and Slack-notify the assigned editor; any node failure logs the error and DMs the team automatically.
The edge — A companion automation reads a Google Drive folder every weekday morning and generates project-management epics and stories from unstructured requirement docs — reported to take a recurring task from days down to hours.

Text a name to a bot, get back a vetted contact in seconds

Rohde & Schwarz · Outbound Sales

"Lead Velocity" — codifying the best rep's instincts into automated sequences

We took what the team's strongest account managers do organically — the right touch, in the right channel, on the right day — and turned it into a repeatable engine that never drops a follow-up.

The opportunity

At a global RF test-and-measurement company, the best account managers ran multi-touch outreach largely from memory. The marketing team wanted that discipline available to everyone — consistent sequencing that still felt account-manager-led, not mass-blasted.

What we built

  • A sequencing engine in Airtable + Make. Sequences, touch templates, enrollments, and call tasks model the entire multi-touch cadence across the territory's account managers.
  • Webhook-driven advancement. When an AM logs a call, a Make scenario looks up the enrollment, finds the next touch, computes the next touch date from the sequence's day-offsets, and advances — or completes — the sequence automatically.
  • Reverse-engineered from the source. We mapped every field and the scheduling logic out of the existing dispatcher to build clean, importable blueprints.
  • A bug caught in the build. The original logic scheduled the next touch off the current touch number rather than the next one — we found and corrected it.
The edge — Six-touch sequences with custom day-offsets that automate a top performer's instincts without losing the human, AM-led feel buyers respond to.

AOC · POST (postmedtech.com)

A governed matchmaking engine for academic MedTech

Not a directory — a controlled, feedback-driven ecosystem where academic departments, MedTech companies, and key opinion leaders are matched, tracked, and held to institutional standards.

The opportunity

AOC needed a trusted way to connect university orthopaedic departments with MedTech and pharma companies — one that preserved institutional alignment, prevented backchanneling, and captured the engagement data from day one. As their CEO put it: if you don't capture the underlying responsiveness and matchmaking data from the beginning, you can never recreate it.

What we built

  • Event-driven automation. Airtable automations fire webhooks into self-hosted n8n, which builds branded payloads and sends transactional email through Brevo — the backbone for every notification in the system.
  • Multi-recipient governance. Application confirmations dedupe and route to the Chair while CC'ing the Program Director and Value Analysis lead — preserving institutional alignment by design.
  • Invite-and-response tracking. Departments receive opportunity invitations with one-click Interested / Not Interested / Need More Info buttons; responses are recorded and a branded confirmation page is served back.
  • Built for the long game. The schema captures a full eight-stage project lifecycle and a reminder cadence (7 / 14 / 21 / 30 days) even while the launch UI exposes only what's needed today.
The edge — Institutions from Cedars-Sinai to Mayo, Yale, and Emory onboarded ahead of a rolling launch — on a platform engineered so credibility is earned through participation, not assumed.

07
Private Real Estate Portfolio · Operations

A property brain that reads its own documents

Upload a lease or a mortgage and AI pulls the key dates and writes a summary — but nothing downstream moves until a human signs off.

The opportunity

A privately held real-estate portfolio was running on an aging FileMaker Pro system. Critical dates — mortgage maturities, lease expirations — lived buried in documents, and missing one is expensive. They needed a single source of truth that surfaced what mattered before it became urgent.

What we built

  • A relational operations model. A 10-table Airtable base with Properties at the center, linked to mortgages, entities, tenants, leases, contacts, documents, and tasks.
  • AI document processing with a human gate. On upload, Airtable's native AI extracts key dates and writes a short summary — then holds it in a pending state. No notification fires until a person reviews and approves it, and a nag email chases anything left pending.
  • An expiry engine in plain language. Mortgage alerts at 18 / 12 / 6 months out, commercial-lease alerts on their own schedule, with labels like "6 months out" instead of raw color codes.
  • A recurring digest. Every fourth Monday at 10am, a recap email lands for the ops team.
The edge — The AI does the reading, the human keeps the authority — an extract-then-approve pipeline that becomes the trusted spine of the entire notification system, replacing a legacy database with no loss of control.

Healthcare Technology Company · Marketing Operations

Replacing enterprise SaaS with a platform the client can actually edit

A 45-person healthcare marketing org's entire planning system, rebuilt from scratch in native Airtable when their commercial tool was sunset — deliberately engineered so their own admin can change anything after we leave.

The opportunity

A healthcare-technology company's marketing department was losing its enterprise roadmapping tool to a company-wide sunset, with a hard deadline and no alternative. They needed a full replacement — fast — that could hold their entire four-tier planning hierarchy and survive without a vendor babysitting it.

What we built

  • A four-tier work hierarchy — Plans → Programs → Tactics → Activities — across 15 tables, with 51 tactic templates that generate their own activities.
  • A sprint engine. Biweekly automation auto-assigns work, carries over and pulls forward tasks, snapshots history, and flags at-risk activities.
  • A business-day date engine. A single consolidated script creates every activity, links prerequisites, computes start and due dates from signed offsets, shifts dependents, and links each activity to the correct sprint — idempotently.
  • AI priority scoring via Airtable AI Agents, with a manual fallback, plus management dashboards for sprint health, time-in-status, and review analytics.
The edge — 101 numbered deliverables, ~45 users, zero custom backend — and built for offboarding: native blocks over vibe-coded shortcuts, every automation self-documented, so the client owns it outright.

Estate Planning & Elder Law Firm · Legal Operations

A multi-system client portal for a law firm

Clio, Airtable, SharePoint, and a 100-user client portal wired into one system — with a hard line that there's no go-live until the data-isolation test passes.

The opportunity

An estate-planning and elder-law firm needed a way to manage client assets and matters across the tools they already lived in — their legal CRM, their document store — plus a secure portal clients could actually use, without anyone ever seeing another client's data.

What we built

  • An asset-management system — a 10-table Airtable base for matters, contacts, assets, tasks, and templated emails, with staff dashboards surfacing missing assets, upcoming meetings, and stalled work.
  • Two-way system sync. Zapier keeps Clio and Airtable in step and routes documents into SharePoint, including a deep-link bridge that writes the Airtable matter URL back into Clio.
  • An attorney recommendation engine that looks up the right templated email by recommendation type and drives a review queue.
  • A secure client portal. A Softr portal sized for ~100 users with 2FA, session timeout, and per-client data isolation.
The edge — 70 numbered deliverables across nine sprint weeks, integrating four systems — with security treated as non-negotiable: no go-live until a cross-client data-isolation stress test passes.
